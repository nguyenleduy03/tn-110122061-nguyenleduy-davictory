=== GENERAL TRAINING TASK 1 — LETTER WRITING ===

For General Training Task 1 (letters), the criterion is called "Task Achievement" but has different meaning from Academic Task 1:

Key letter grading factors:
- PURPOSE: Is the purpose of the letter clear and maintained throughout?
- TONE: Is the tone appropriate for the letter type (formal/semi-formal/informal)?
- BULLET POINTS: Are all required bullet points addressed and expanded?
- FORMAT: Is the letter format correct (greeting, body, closing)?

Letter type characteristics:
- Formal: Dear Sir/Madam → Yours faithfully. No contractions. Polite, respectful language.
- Semi-formal: Dear Mr/Ms [Name] → Best regards. Some contractions OK. Professional but courteous.
- Informal: Dear [Name] → Love/Best wishes. Contractions OK. Friendly, conversational tone.

=== TASK RESPONSE PENALTY RULES ===

Penalty factors (reduce or cap when present):
- Ideas are stated but NOT explained
- Examples are generic — could apply to any topic
- Arguments repeat the same point in different words
- Missing development chain: Claim → Explanation → Example → Result
- One body paragraph is significantly underdeveloped

Band ceilings:
- Shallow ideas or limited development → max TR 6.0
- Ideas developed but examples generic → max TR 7.0
- Ideas well-developed but lack precision → max TR 8.0
- Only TR 9 when ideas are fully developed, precise, and persuasive throughout

=== COHERENCE & COHESION PENALTY RULES ===

Penalty factors:
- Over-use of cohesive devices
- Every sentence starts with Firstly, Secondly, Moreover, Furthermore, In addition, Besides, etc.
- Mechanical linking
- Abrupt topic shifts between sentences
- Paragraphs without clear topic sentences

Band ceilings:
- Mechanical cohesion → max CC 6.0
- Reasonable cohesion but still noticeable → max CC 7.0
- Natural cohesion but not yet skilful → max CC 8.0

=== LEXICAL RESOURCE PENALTY RULES ===

Assess four dimensions: Range, Precision, Collocation, Paraphrasing.

Penalty factors:
- Key words repeated excessively
- Same vocabulary structure repeated
- Mostly B1 level vocabulary
- Academic words used unnaturally
- Incorrect collocations

Band ceilings:
- Mostly basic vocabulary → max LR 6.0
- Some less common words but used inflexibly → max LR 7.0
- Wide range but occasionally unnatural → max LR 8.0

Real vocabulary calibration:
- Band 6: good, bad, important, useful, many
- Band 7: financial security, long-term goals, unexpected situations
- Band 8: economic uncertainty, accumulate capital, financial resilience
- Band 9: sophisticated, natural, highly precise vocabulary throughout

=== GRAMMATICAL RANGE & ACCURACY PENALTY RULES ===

Assess: Sentence variety, Complex structures, Accuracy, Flexibility.

Penalty factors:
- Too many simple sentences
- Only uses S + V + O patterns
- Same sentence type repeated
- Article errors
- Preposition errors
- Subject-verb agreement errors
- Punctuation errors

Band ceilings:
- Mostly simple sentences → max GRA 6.0
- Some complex sentences but limited variety → max GRA 7.0
- Varied structures but still minor errors → max GRA 8.0
- Band 9 only when: full range, near-native flexibility, only rare slips

=== REAL IELTS CALIBRATION ===

The following factors do NOT automatically lift an essay to Band 7:
- Has an introduction
- Has a conclusion
- Has 4-5 paragraphs
- Has examples
- Has counterarguments
- Has no major grammar errors

An essay only reaches Band 7+ when:
- Ideas are clearly developed with depth
- Arguments show causal or consequential reasoning
- Vocabulary and grammar clearly exceed intermediate level

=== COMMON SCORE CEILINGS ===

If vocabulary is mostly B1 level → overall max 6.5
If examples are all generic → TR max 7.0
If mostly simple sentences → GRA max 6.0
If cohesion is mechanical → CC max 6.5
If essay reads like a template → overall max 7.0

=== OFFICIAL BAND DESCRIPTORS (REFERENCE) ===

Band 7:
- Addresses all parts of the task; clear position throughout
- Main ideas are extended and supported
- Logically organised with clear progression
- Sufficient range of vocabulary with some less common items
- Variety of complex structures with frequent error-free sentences

Band 8:
- Sufficiently addresses all parts; ideas relevant, well-developed, supported
- Arguments logically developed; counterarguments handled effectively where relevant
- Logical sequencing with well-managed cohesion
- Wide range of vocabulary used fluently and flexibly
- Wide range of structures; majority of sentences error-free

Band 9:
- Fully addresses all parts; fully developed position
- Ideas fully extended with precise, sophisticated support
- Effortless cohesion and skilful paragraphing
- Natural, sophisticated vocabulary control
- Full range with full flexibility and accuracy; rare 'slip' errors
